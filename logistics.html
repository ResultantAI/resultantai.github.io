Common Questions

How long does implementation take?

4 weeks from kickoff to live system. We handle the data migration, configure the system for your workflow, and train your team.

Will my drivers and loader operators need to learn new technology?

The tablet/mobile interface is simpler than a GPS. If they can use a smartphone, they can use this. Most teams are fully trained in 30 minutes.

What happens when the internet goes down? Do trucks stop rolling?

No. Your trucks never stop. The system is "offline-first." Tablets work independently - drivers create tickets, capture signatures, and print receipts with zero internet connection. You could cut the fiber line to your office and drivers would still haul rock. Data syncs automatically in the background when connectivity returns. This was designed for quarries, pits, and rural delivery routes where cell service doesn't exist.

Can this integrate with QuickBooks? Do I lose control?

Yes, with a critical safety feature: You review and approve every ticket before it syncs to QuickBooks. The system creates a review queue where you verify quantities, apply your pricing rules, and check for errors. With one click, approved tickets sync to QuickBooks with the correct customer, class tracking, and line items. You stay in control of your books.

What's the investment?

Depends on fleet size and features needed. Most systems pay for themselves within 60-90 days through eliminated lost tickets and labor savings. Book a call for a custom quote based on your operation.

Do you work with companies our size?

We specialize in 3-50 truck operations. Big enough to feel the pain of manual processes, small enough to implement fast.
